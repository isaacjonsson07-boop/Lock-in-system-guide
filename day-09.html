Your Priorities

You've defined who you are. Now you define what matters most. Not everything can be a priority — and treating everything equally is the fastest way to make progress on nothing.

The Law of Concentration

The ability to concentrate single-mindedly on the most important thing and stay with it until it is complete is the hallmark of high achievement.

Scattered effort produces scattered results. Concentrated effort produces breakthroughs.

The priority problem

Most people have too many priorities — which means they have none. When everything is important, nothing gets the focused attention it needs to actually move forward.

You know this already. You've felt the pull of trying to improve everything at once — health, career, relationships, finances, learning, habits — and ending each week feeling like you barely moved on any of them.

The fix isn't working harder. It's choosing fewer things and going deeper on them.

How to build your priority stack

A priority stack is a ranked list of the areas that get your focused energy. Not all the areas of your life — just the ones that are active priorities right now.

Step 1: List your life domains. Career, health, relationships, finances, personal development, creativity — whatever categories are relevant to your life.

Step 2: Choose your top 3. Which three domains, if you made real progress on them in the next 14 days, would create the biggest positive impact on your direction? Those are your active priorities.

Step 3: Define one clear outcome for each. Not "improve my health" but "exercise 4 times per week and prepare meals in advance." Specific, measurable, tied to a behavior.

Everything else goes on the maintenance list — you handle it, but you don't invest focused energy in it during this installation period.

What to do today

Complete these before moving to Day 10.

List all your life domains
Write down every area of your life that competes for your attention. Be complete — most people have 6–10.
Choose your top 3 active priorities
Which three areas would create the most meaningful progress toward your direction if you focused on them over the next 14 days? Rank them.
Define one clear outcome for each priority
For each of your top 3, write one specific, observable outcome you're working toward. Not a feeling — a result.
Create your "not now" list
Everything that didn't make the top 3 goes here. This isn't abandonment — it's intentional deferral. Write it down so your brain can let go of it.

You now have direction, identity, and a clear priority stack. These three together form the strategic core of your system — they determine what you focus on, how you show up, and where your energy flows.

From today forward, every decision gets filtered through your top 3 priorities. If it doesn't serve them, it waits.
