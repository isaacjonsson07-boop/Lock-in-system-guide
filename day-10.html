The Law of Practice

Any thought, feeling, or behavior that you repeat often enough eventually becomes automatic and requires no further effort.

Habits are how you turn intention into infrastructure.

From non-negotiables to habits

On Day 4, you set non-negotiables — the minimum behaviors that keep your system alive. Today we go further. You're designing 2–3 keystone habits that actively drive progress on your top priorities.

A keystone habit is one that creates a chain reaction. When you do it, other good things follow naturally. Exercise often improves sleep, energy, and focus. A morning planning session often improves productivity for the entire day.

You don't need ten habits. You need the right two or three, installed properly.

The habit installation formula

Every habit has three parts:

Cue — the trigger that starts the behavior. A time, a location, a preceding action, or a feeling. The more specific the cue, the more automatic the habit becomes.

Routine — the behavior itself. Keep it small enough that resistance is minimal. You can always expand later. Right now, consistency matters more than intensity.

Reward — the payoff that tells your brain this was worth doing. It doesn't have to be elaborate. The satisfaction of checking it off in your tracker is often enough.

Vague intention
"I'm going to start exercising more and reading every day."
Installed habit
"After my morning anchor (cue), I do a 20-minute workout (routine), then log it in my tracker (reward). After my evening close-out (cue), I read for 15 minutes (routine), then mark the day complete (reward)."

The first is a wish. The second is a system. The cue-routine-reward structure makes it repeatable without willpower.

What to do today

Complete these before moving to Day 11.

Choose 2–3 keystone habits tied to your priorities
For each of your top 3 priorities, identify one habit that would drive the most progress. Pick the 2–3 that feel most impactful.
Design each one using cue-routine-reward
Write out the specific cue, routine, and reward for each habit. Be precise. "After [cue], I [routine], then [reward]."
Keep the routines small
Each routine should take 15–30 minutes or less. If it feels like a lot, shrink it. Consistency beats intensity. You can scale up after it's automatic.
Add your new habits to the tracker
Update your tracker with these keystone habits alongside your non-negotiables. From today, you're tracking both.
