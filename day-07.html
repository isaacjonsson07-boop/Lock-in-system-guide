The Law of Feedback

Success is not a straight line — it's a process of continuous course correction based on honest feedback.

The review doesn't judge you. It informs you.

Why reviews matter more than lessons

Information without reflection is entertainment. You've absorbed six days of principles, built real structures, and started tracking. But none of that matters if you don't stop and honestly assess what actually happened.

This review is not about motivation. It's not about feeling good or bad about your week. It's a structured diagnostic — a 15-minute process that tells you exactly where your system is strong and where it needs work.

Get used to this. Reviews are the most valuable part of the entire system. They're what separate people who complete things from people who just start them.

How to run your review

Open your tracker. Look at the past 6 days. Then answer these questions honestly:

Direction check — Is your direction statement still clear and relevant? Does it still motivate action when you read it? If not, sharpen it.

Structure check — How many days did you follow your daily structure? What caused the misses? Is the structure realistic or does it need adjusting?

Non-negotiable check — Which ones did you hit consistently? Which ones did you skip? Why?

Default check — Did any of the old patterns from Day 3 resurface? How did you respond?

Environment check — Did the changes you made yesterday help? What still needs adjusting?

What to do today

Complete these before moving to Day 8.

Run a full 6-day review
Open your tracker and review each day. For each component (direction, structure, non-negotiables), rate your consistency on a simple scale: strong, partial, or missed.
Identify your #1 strength and #1 weakness
What held up best this week? What fell apart most? Be specific — not "discipline" but "I skipped my morning anchor 3 out of 6 days because I stayed up too late."
Recalibrate one thing
Based on your review, make one adjustment. Tighten a non-negotiable, simplify your structure, sharpen your direction statement. One change, implemented tomorrow.
Write a one-paragraph status report
Summarize your first week in 3–5 sentences. Where are you now compared to Day 1? This becomes part of your installation record.
